# floral-tea-drinks-rise sources

## Topic
- Floral aroma as a primary branding and product-architecture language in 2026 Chinese new tea drinks
- Main contrast set used during topic selection:
  - light milk tea as category structure
  - fruit tea as seasonal visibility engine
  - matcha as threshold/maturity signal
  - floral tea drinks as scent-based brand identity system

## Key source pages consulted
1. Molly Tea official site (brand overview)
   - https://cn.mollytea.com/
   - Signals used:
     - positions itself as a flower-tea leader
     - “Eastern modern” aesthetic language
     - store scale reference

2. Molly Tea official site (product categories)
   - https://cn.mollytea.com/product-categories-cn/
   - Signals used:
     - floral fresh tea / floral cream-top / floral ice tea / floral fruit tea as menu architecture
     - product examples built around jasmine, magnolia, gardenia, osmanthus, matcha-floral hybrids

3. Chagee official site (fresh milk tea series)
   - https://www.chagee.com/zh-cn/product/fresh-milk-tea-series?autoscroll=lists

     - increasingly specific tea-base and floral naming

4. Chagee official site (fruit tea series)

   - https://www.chagee.com/zh-cn/product/iced-oriental-tea-series?autoscroll=lists

## Selection rationale
- Rejected as too close to existing coverage:
  - another light milk tea framing
  - another fruit tea seasonal rebound framing
  - another Chagee-only brand rise framing
  - another matcha cycle framing
- Chosen because it is adjacent to several existing topics but not reducible to any of them:
  - central thesis is floral aroma as brand system, not floral drinks as a flavor trend alone
  - strongest differentiator is scent-based identity and scalable menu architecture
